\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Phase 1: Single Mess3 Verification}

We first verify our setup on a single Mess3 ($\alpha = 0.85$, $x = 0.05$; 3 layers, $d_{\text{model}} = 64$, 10K steps). Training loss converges to $0.82$ nats, matching the Bayes-optimal CE of $0.82$. Linear regression (held-out 80/20 split) from final-layer activations to belief states achieves $R^2 = 0.989$ (all positions aggregated; $R^2 = 0.947$ at the last position only). The Sierpinski gasket is clearly recovered (Figure~\ref{fig:p2_gaskets}, left column is analogous). Effective dimensionality decreases from 17 to 6 over training.

\subsection{Phase 2: Non-Ergodic Mixture}

We train a 4-layer model ($d_{\text{model}} = 128$) on the 3-component mixture (Table~\ref{tab:components}) for 5K steps (loss converges by step $\sim$2K).

\paragraph{Belief regression.} All three component-specific gaskets are recovered with high fidelity (Table~\ref{tab:regression}). The meta-belief (posterior over components) is linearly decodable with $R^2 = 0.941$. Figure~\ref{fig:p2_gaskets} shows ground-truth vs.\ predicted gaskets.

\paragraph{Dimensionality.} Effective dimensionality (95\% CEV) converges to 6, between the factored prediction ($K{+}1 = 4$) and joint prediction ($3K{-}1 = 8$). This suggests partial factoring with additional dimensions for positional encoding.

\paragraph{Per-position loss.} The model tracks the Bayesian baseline closely (Figure~\ref{fig:p2_loss}), remaining above the oracle baseline. The gap quantifies the cost of imperfect meta-synchronization. Near-optimal inference is achieved by position $\sim$6.
