\section{Background}

\paragraph{Mess3 process.} A 3-state nonunifilar HMM emitting $z \in \{0,1,2\}$, parameterized by emission clarity $\alpha$ and state-switching rate $x$, with $\beta=(1{-}\alpha)/2$, $y=1{-}2x$. The labeled transition matrices $T^{(z)}_{ij} = P(\text{emit } z, \text{go to } j \mid \text{state } i)$ are given in Appendix~\ref{app:mess3}. The process is nonunifilar: observing a token does not uniquely determine the state transition, so optimal prediction requires maintaining a belief distribution over hidden states. These belief states form a Sierpinski gasket in the 2-simplex \cite{riechers2018spectral}. The subdominant eigenvalue $\zeta = 1{-}3x$ controls the exponential decay rate of belief corrections with context distance. The entropy rate $H = -\sum_s \pi(s) \sum_z P(z|s) \log P(z|s)$ with uniform $\pi(s) = 1/3$ gives the theoretical minimum cross-entropy.

\paragraph{Belief geometry in transformers.} Transformers linearly represent belief states $\veta^{(z_{1:t})} = \veta^{(\varnothing)} T^{(z_1)} \cdots T^{(z_t)} / \text{norm}$ in their residual stream \cite{shai2024transformers}. This representation is a constrained Bayesian approximation: $r_1(z_{1:d}) = \bpi + \sum_{s=1}^{d} (\bpi T^{z_s} T^{d-s} - \bpi)$, where each correction decays as $\zeta^{d-s}$, reflecting the parallel nature of attention \cite{piotrowski2025constrained}.

\paragraph{Factored World Hypothesis.} Beyond single processes, transformers have an inductive bias toward factored representations \cite{shai2026factored}: conditionally independent factors are represented in orthogonal subspaces, requiring $\sum_n (d_n {-} 1)$ dimensions vs.\ $\prod_n d_n {-} 1$ (joint). In synthetic experiments with 5 factors (3 Mess3 + 2 Bloch Walk, joint vocab 432), activations converge to 10 dimensions (factored) rather than 242 (joint). This factoring persists even when it sacrifices fidelity, acting as a representational attractor that models depart from only under sustained gradient pressure.

\paragraph{ICL as source discrimination.} Non-ergodicity forces hierarchical inference: identify the source, then predict within it. This is structurally identical to in-context learning in LLMs \cite{shai2026factored}. ICL and activation steering both modify the model's belief state about latent concepts, operating additively in log-belief space \cite{bigelow2025belief}.
