\section{Introduction}

Language models face non-ergodic data where different documents constitute distinct statistical regimes. In-context learning requires identifying the active regime before predicting optimally. We study this in a controlled setting using Mess3 processes \cite{marzen2017nearly, shai2024transformers}, 3-state nonunifilar HMMs whose belief geometry forms a Sierpinski gasket.

By constructing a non-ergodic training set where each sequence comes from one of $K$ Mess3 parameterizations, we create a minimal in-context learning problem. This connects to the Factored World Hypothesis \cite{shai2026factored}: component identity and within-component belief could be represented in orthogonal subspaces with dimensionality $(K-1) + 2 = K+1$, rather than the joint $3K - 1$. However, the joint transition operator is block-diagonal (not tensor-product), creating an intermediate case.

\paragraph{Contributions.} (1) We formalize the non-ergodic mixture as hierarchical belief inference and derive geometric predictions (\S\ref{sec:predictions}). (2) We verify single-process gasket recovery ($R^2 = 0.989$) and analyze the mixture against the Bayes-optimal predictor (\S\ref{sec:results}). (3) We analyze synchronization dynamics: when and where the model identifies the active component (\S\ref{sec:additional}).
